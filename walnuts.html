After Oil Extraction: Uses of Walnut By-Products

Uses of Walnut Wastage Description
Animal Feed High in protein and fiber, walnut meal is a nutritious supplement for livestock feed.
Walnut Flour/Meal for Baking Gluten-free, low-carb ingredient suitable for baking and adding texture to foods.
Compost and Organic Fertilizer Walnut husks and shells can be composted to enrich soil with organic matter and nutrients.
Biofuel and Biomass Production Walnut shells and husks can be converted into biofuel, providing a renewable energy source.
Cosmetic Exfoliants Walnut shell powder is often used as a natural exfoliant in skincare products like scrubs.
Food Industry Additives Ground walnut meal is added to protein bars, granola, and yogurt for added fiber and protein.
Aquaculture Feed Walnut by-products serve as a sustainable and protein-rich feed option for fish farming.
Biodegradable Packaging Walnut shell materials can be processed into eco-friendly, biodegradable packaging.
Smoothies and Sauces Leftover walnut pulp can be added to smoothies and sauces to increase texture and nutrient content.
Pet Food Ingredient Provides a nutritious and high-fiber additive in pet foods for dogs and cats.
Activated Carbon Production Walnut shells can be processed into activated carbon, useful for water and air filtration.
Mulch for Gardening Ground shells act as a natural mulch, helping retain soil moisture and control weeds.
Soil Erosion Prevention Walnut shells in erosion-prone areas can help stabilize soil and prevent runoff.

Profit Calculation

Using a range approach (minimum to maximum):

Total Revenue (including by-products):
  • Minimum: ₹6,00,000 + ₹20,000 = ₹6,20,000
  • Maximum: ₹12,00,000 + ₹40,000 = ₹12,40,000

Net Profit Calculation:
  • Minimum Profit: ₹6,20,000 - ₹4,70,000 = ₹1,50,000
