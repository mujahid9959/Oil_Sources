Soil Requirements Corn thrives in fertile, well-drained loamy soil with a pH range of 5.8 to 7.0. Good organic matter and soil aeration are beneficial for healthy root development.
Climate Corn grows best in warm conditions with an ideal temperature range of 18°C to 27°C. It is highly sensitive to frost, making it suitable for spring and summer planting.
Planting Density Typically planted at a density of 10,000-12,000 plants per acre for optimal growth and yield. Close spacing encourages a high crop density, improving productivity.
Water Requirements Requires regular watering, especially during germination and silking stages. Corn needs about 20-25 inches of water throughout its growing season.
Nutrient Requirements Corn is a heavy feeder and responds well to nitrogen, phosphorous, and potassium fertilizers. Micronutrients like zinc can also enhance crop growth.
Pest and Disease Management Common pests include corn earworm, armyworm, and aphids. Diseases like maize rust, leaf blight, and smut are controlled through crop rotation and fungicides.
Harvesting Method Harvested when kernels are dry and hard, usually at 18-20% moisture content. Corn can be hand-harvested or mechanically harvested, depending on farm size.
Post-Harvest Processing Dried kernels can be stored, milled into cornmeal, or processed for various by-products. Proper drying and storage prevent mold and preserve quality.
Market Demand Corn has high demand in food, animal feed, and biofuel industries. It is also used in industrial products, including plastics, adhesives, and biopolymers.
Nutritional Benefits High in fiber, vitamins (especially B vitamins), and essential minerals. Corn also contains antioxidants, such as carotenoids, which promote eye health.
Uses of Corn Products Used in human food (cornmeal, corn syrup), animal feed, biofuel (ethanol), and in various industrial applications like cornstarch and corn-based bioplastics.
Environmental Impact Large-scale corn farming requires careful nutrient management to avoid soil degradation. Crop rotation with legumes improves soil health and reduces pest buildup.
Storage and Shelf Life Properly dried and stored corn can last up to a year without spoilage. Corn kernels are often stored in silos at low moisture to prevent mold growth.

Profit Calculation

Using a range approach (minimum to maximum):

Total Revenue (including by-products):
  • Minimum: ₹30,000 + ₹10,000 = ₹40,000
  • Maximum: ₹80,000 + ₹30,000 = ₹1,10,000
Total Costs (initial + maintenance):
  • Minimum: ₹1,50,000 + ₹40,000 = ₹1,90,000
  • Maximum: ₹3,00,000 + ₹70,000 = ₹3,70,000
Net Profit Calculation:
  • Minimum Profit: ₹40,000 - ₹1,90,000 = -₹1,50,000 (loss)
  • Maximum Profit: ₹1,10,000 - ₹3,70,000 = -₹2,60,000 (loss)
