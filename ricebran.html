Details Of Ricebran

Aspect Details
Initial Cost/Acre ₹10,000 - ₹20,000
Maintenance Cost ₹15,000 - ₹25,000 per year
Harvest Time 3-4 months (Two crop cycles: June-August and November-February)
Yield/Acre 2,500 - 3,500 kg (Rice yield; bran yield is ~10% of rice yield)
Rice Bran Revenue/Acre ₹8,000 - ₹12,000
Net Profit from Rice Bran/Acre ₹5,000 - ₹9,000 per year
Total Wastage 5-10% (post-milling)
Water Requirement 10,000 - 15,000 cubic meters per acre per crop cycle
Labor Requirement 2-4 workers per acre, with peak labor during planting and harvesting
Soil Requirements Clayey to loamy soil with good water retention and pH 5.5 - 6.5
Irrigation Frequency Requires consistent water; ideally irrigated paddy fields
Fertilization NPK (Nitrogen-Phosphorus-Potassium) and organic manure recommended
Pest Control Regular monitoring; pest control for leaf folder, stem borer, and brown planthopper
Harvest Method Manual or mechanized harvesting depending on farm size
Storage Requirements Cool, dry storage to prevent mold and maintain grain quality
Market Demand High demand for rice bran in oil extraction, animal feed, and food industry
Climate Suitability Warm, humid climate; ideal temperatures 20-37°C
Rice Bran Uses Oil extraction, animal feed, cosmetic ingredients, and dietary supplements
Return on Investment (ROI) Typically 20-30% for rice bran profit alone
Environmental Impact High water usage; runoff management and organic methods can reduce impact
Post-harvest Processing Milling and drying required for rice; bran requires further processing for oil extraction

After Oil Extraction: Uses of Ricebran By-Products

Uses of Ricebran Wastage Description
Animal Feed High in protein and fiber, rice bran meal is commonly used as a supplement in livestock feed.
Aquaculture Feed Rice bran by-products are suitable as an ingredient in fish and shrimp feed for aquaculture.
Biofuel Production Can be converted into biofuels, providing a renewable energy source.
Compost and Organic Fertilizer Enriches soil with nutrients when used as compost, enhancing soil quality for agriculture.
Pharmaceutical Industry Used in supplements for its antioxidants, fiber, and essential fatty acids.
Cosmetic Products Incorporated into skin care for its antioxidants, acting as a natural moisturizer and exfoliant.
Dietary Fiber Additive Adds dietary fiber to health products such as cereals, bars, and functional foods.
Pet Food Ingredient Provides a nutritious, high-fiber component in pet foods, especially for dogs and cats.
Biodegradable Packaging Processed into eco-friendly, biodegradable packaging materials, reducing plastic use.
Fermentation Industry Used as a substrate in fermentation processes for producing enzymes, alcohols, and other products.
Biochar Production Can be converted into biochar, which is used for soil improvement and carbon sequestration.
Rice Bran Wax Extracted for use in cosmetics and candle-making as a natural, sustainable wax.
Building Material Additive Used in making eco-friendly building materials like bricks and particle boards.
Human Food Additive Added to foods for additional nutrition, especially as a gluten-free, high-fiber supplement.
Mulch for Gardening Acts as an effective mulch for gardens, helping retain moisture and suppress weeds.
Soil Stabilization Used in erosion control and soil stabilization projects, especially in construction sites.

Example Calculation

  1. Initial Cost per Acre: ₹10,000 - ₹20,000
  2. Maintenance Cost per Acre per Year: ₹15,000 - ₹25,000
  3. Rice Bran Revenue per Acre per Year: ₹8,000 - ₹12,000
  4. Other Potential Income (from By-Products): Revenue from by-products such as animal feed, biofuel, compost, contributing ₹5,000 - ₹10,000 per year.

Assumption: By-products contribute an additional ₹5,000 - ₹10,000 in revenue per year.

Profit Calculation

Using a range approach (minimum to maximum):

Total Revenue (including by-products):
  • Minimum: ₹8,000 + ₹5,000 = ₹13,000
  • Maximum: ₹12,000 + ₹10,000 = ₹22,000
Total Costs (initial + maintenance):
  • Minimum: ₹10,000 + ₹15,000 = ₹25,000
  • Maximum: ₹20,000 + ₹25,000 = ₹45,000
Net Profit Calculation:
  • Minimum Profit: ₹13,000 - ₹45,000 = -₹32,000 (loss)
  • Maximum Profit: ₹22,000 - ₹25,000 = ₹3,000
